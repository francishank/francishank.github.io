Types of Coffee Beans

There are primarily four main types of coffee beans that are widely recognized and cultivated for commercial use. These are Arabica, Robusta, Liberica, and Excelsa. Here’s a breakdown of each:

  1. Arabica (Coffea arabica):

    • Origins: Indigenous to the forests of the southwestern highlands of Ethiopia.
    • Description: Known for its smooth, mild flavor with slightly acidic tones and a range of nuanced flavors depending on the region of cultivation.
    • Caffeine Content: Generally contains less caffeine than Robusta.
    • Market Share: Represents about 60-70% of the world’s coffee production.

  2. Robusta (Coffea canephora):

    • Origins: Originally from central and western sub-Saharan Africa.
    • Description: Has a stronger, harsher taste with a grain-like overtone and higher caffeine content compared to Arabica.
    • Caffeine Content: Typically contains around 2.2-2.7% caffeine content.
    • Market Share: Accounts for about 30-40% of the global coffee production.

  3. Liberica (Coffea liberica):

    • Origins: Native to Liberia, West Africa.
    • Description: Known for its unique, somewhat fruity and floral flavor profile with a woody or smoky aroma.
    • Market Share: Represents a very small percentage of the world’s coffee production, primarily grown in West Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

  4. Excelsa (Coffea excelsa):

    • Origins: Originally from West Africa.
    • Description: Exhibits a unique, complex flavor profile with fruity and tart notes, often used in blends to add depth.
    • Market Share: Represents a minor portion of global coffee production compared to Arabica and Robusta.

Here’s a simplified table showcasing some key attributes of each type:

Coffee Bean Type Flavor Profile Caffeine Content Market Share
Arabica Smooth, mild, nuanced Lower 60-70%
Robusta Strong, harsh, grainy Higher 30-40%
Liberica Fruity, floral, woody Moderate Very small
Excelsa Complex, fruity, tart Moderate Minor portion

These figures and descriptions may vary slightly depending on factors such as growing conditions, processing methods, and regional variations.
